About Jennifer Westwood

From the choir loft to the corner bar, Jennifer Westwood found her voice somewhere in the middle. A product of Detroit, she grew up listening to Motor City gospel, and also nurtured a fondness for her roots—a heritage filled with country music musicians.

Singing in church landed her many studio gigs on various local jazz, hip-hop and R&B projects and lead to her first public singing experiences, including a gig singing back up for Motown artist Carl Carlton (Bad Mama Jama) and singing the national anthem at a Detroit Lions opening day game.
